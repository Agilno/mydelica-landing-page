We collect and store this information using cookies. A cookie is a small text file stored on your computer to identify your device, help you navigate our site or prepare customized Web pages and save site login information for you. This means that the next time you log in we can remember who you are and adapt to your preferences.

We may use both “session ID cookies” and “persistent cookies.” A session cookie lets our website recognize you as you move from page to page so that you are not treated as a new visitor every time you click on a new internal link.

A persistent cookie allows our website to understand your preferences, settings and information so you can gain rapid access to your preferred subjects at your next visit.

You can disable cookies through your browser. If you do so, however, some features of the website that rely on cookies will no longer be available to you.
